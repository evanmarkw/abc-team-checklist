‚ö° TIPS FROM THE CMC COMMITTEE (From FAQ Document)

TIP #1: Read and follow the RFP exactly. If it specifies a format, font, or requirement, there's probably scoring criteria tied to it. Make a checklist! ‚úì
TIP #2: Reach out to your local ABC Chapter for help finding contractors willing to provide feedback and mentoring. They can't do the work for you, but they can review it.
TIP #3: Reach out to alumni and faculty for peer reviews and feedback.
TIP #4: Step 1 proposal can be worked on by active ABC Student Chapter members beyond your 4-person team! However, Step 2 & 3 are solely the 4-person team's responsibility.
TIP #5: Meet with local contractors to review their actual RFP responses for inspiration on format and quality.
TIP #6: Be cautious of internet research. Public cost/schedule data may not reflect the RFP-specific scope changes and will likely lead you astray.
TIP #7: Your team is a "company." Get creative! Create a company name, logo, team resumes/experience, portfolio. Have fun with it.
TIP #8: Develop boilerplate drafts early and get multiple peer reviews done well before deadline.
TIP #9: Don't use canned Safety/QC plans from the internet, AI, or another company. Don't just reuse last year's submission. Make it specific to THIS project and better overall.
TIP #10: When the RFP says "but not limited to..." that's code for: "Put some thought into this and include additional items beyond what's listed here."
TIP #11: Think outside the box. Top safety risks don't have to be OSHA's Fatal Four. Consider a daycare across the street full of kids - how will you mitigate THAT risk?
TIP #12: Begin with the end in mind. Come planning to shortlist and present. Have your Step 3 presentation at least STARTED before you arrive. If you make the cut, you'll be prepared and ahead of others.
TIP #14: Rehearse your presentation in front of peers, contractors, anyone who can give feedback and grill you with questions. Can't find someone? Contact your ABC Chapter liaison or the CMC Committee for help.
TIP #15: If possible, have someone from 2027's team shadow you this year. Start planning for next year NOW.
TIP #16: Teams that don't make the Step 3 shortlist should attend the interviews anyway. This provides ideas for improvement and insight into the interview process.
